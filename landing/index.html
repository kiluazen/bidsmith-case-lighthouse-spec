why teams switch

Specs that read like a senior PM wrote them, not like a meeting transcript.

Most product docs leak ambiguity into engineering. Engineers ask the same questions in standup for two weeks while the spec sits half-edited. Lighthouse collapses that loop.

01

Names the goal in one sentence.

The spec opens with a single, testable goal sentence — not three paragraphs of context. If we can't write it, you don't have a spec yet.

02

Pins risks before scope.

Three honest risks at the top, before the bullet list of features. Most specs hide their risks at the bottom; engineers find them on day 4.

03

Stops at "day-1 scope".

Every spec is constrained to what ships in the first deployable cut. Out-of-scope items go to a separate "later" section, by name, by why.

how it works

From "ugh, write the spec" to "ok, post it" in 4 minutes.

i

Drop in your Notion link, or paste raw notes.

We read meeting notes, transcripts, Slack snippets, screenshots, half-finished docs. Messier the better.

ii

Pick the spec shape.

Day-1 scope, full PRD, RFC, or "engineer brief". Each shape has its own house style — and the house style is opinionated.

iii

Lighthouse drafts. You red-pen.

You see the draft inline next to the source. Click any sentence to surface the source paragraph. Edit, accept, ship.

iv

Push to Notion, Linear, or Markdown.

Native sync to Notion + Linear, or a clean Markdown export. The spec is owned by you, not by us.
